Running a restaurant in Denver means wearing every hat at once. The things that consistently fall through the cracks - review requests, loyalty follow-up, catering inquiry responses, no-show follow-ups - are the exact things AI handles well. Here is what Denver restaurant owners are actually using AI for in 2026.

## Review collection

The single highest-ROI automation for most Denver restaurants is automated review requests. After a dine-in visit, delivery order, or catering event, an automated text or email asks for a Google review with a direct link. Most customers are happy to leave one if you ask immediately after a good experience.

The difference between a 4.1 and a 4.7 Google rating in a competitive Denver neighborhood can determine whether a new customer chooses you or the place next door.

---

## Loyalty and reactivation

Customers who visited 60 days ago and have not been back are the easiest customers to reactivate - they already know you. An automated sequence that sends a "we miss you" message with a small offer or update costs nothing to run and consistently brings people back.

Denver has a highly local dining culture. People want to support neighborhood spots. A simple reactivation message is often all it takes.

---

## Catering and event inquiries

Most catering inquiries come in outside business hours. An AI that immediately acknowledges the inquiry, asks the right qualifying questions (date, headcount, budget, dietary restrictions), and routes hot leads to the right person means you never lose a catering booking to a competitor just because you responded slower.

---

## What AI does not replace

Your chef, your service staff, your hospitality - none of that gets automated. AI handles the admin and follow-through so your team focuses on the experience.
