Front desk overhead, no-shows, and patients who fall out of the recall cycle are the three biggest operational drains on Denver medical and dental practices. AI handles all three without adding staff.

## Appointment reminders that actually work

A single reminder call the day before is no longer enough. The practices with the lowest no-show rates are running a multi-touch sequence: an automated text 72 hours out, a confirmation request 24 hours out, and a same-day reminder. Patients who do not confirm get a call flag so staff can fill the slot.

For a Denver dental practice doing 30+ appointments a day, reducing no-shows by even 15% is a significant revenue recovery.

---

## Patient recall automation

The recall cycle is where most practices leak patients without realizing it. Patients who are 6 months overdue for a cleaning, 12 months past their last physical, or due for a follow-up on a chronic condition need a nudge - not a manual call from your front desk.

An automated recall sequence that sends a text, offers online booking, and follows up once more if they do not schedule converts a steady stream of lapsed patients back into the chair without manual effort.

---

## New patient onboarding

The first impression starts before a patient arrives. An automated onboarding sequence that sends intake forms in advance, explains what to bring, gives parking and check-in instructions, and follows up with a confirmation reduces first-visit friction and saves 10-15 minutes of in-office admin time per new patient.

---

## Post-visit follow-up

A check-in message 48 hours after a procedure or treatment both improves patient satisfaction and catches complications early. For dental practices specifically, a post-procedure follow-up sequence reduces anxiety calls and builds the patient relationship.

---

## What AI does not do in a medical context

AI handles communication workflows - reminders, recalls, follow-ups. It does not make clinical decisions, handle sensitive health information in unauthorized ways, or replace clinical staff judgment. The automations we build are communication and scheduling workflows, not clinical tools.
