If you run a contracting business in Denver - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, remodeling - you already know the pattern: you're great at the actual work, and the admin around it is a constant drag.

Estimates sitting in your inbox. Leads that came in over the weekend and didn't hear back until Monday. Customers texting for job status updates you don't have time to answer. Follow-up that just... doesn't happen.

This is where AI is genuinely useful for contractors. Not for the job itself - for everything around it.

---

## The follow-up problem

The biggest revenue leak for most contractors isn't bad jobs - it's slow or missing follow-up. Research consistently shows that the first business to respond to a lead wins the job more often than the best-priced one.

Most contractors respond to new leads hours later, or the next morning if it comes in after hours. That gap is where jobs go to competitors.

AI changes this. A new inquiry comes in at 10pm - AI sends a response immediately, asks the right qualifying questions, and books a call or site visit directly onto your calendar. You wake up to a scheduled appointment, not a cold lead.

---

## What else contractors are automating

**Estimate follow-up.** You send an estimate and then forget to follow up. AI handles this - it sends the follow-up on your behalf at the right intervals, flags if the prospect hasn't responded, and keeps you from losing jobs you already quoted.

**Job completion follow-up.** Reviews and referrals come from asking. Most contractors don't ask consistently because they're onto the next job. AI sends the follow-up after job completion automatically.

**Customer status updates.** "When's my job starting?" - AI can answer these based on your schedule, keeping customers from calling your cell for updates.

**Scheduling and reminders.** Confirmation texts, reminder messages the day before, prep instructions - all automatic.

---

## What this looks like in practice

One HVAC company in the Denver metro was losing roughly 30% of after-hours leads to faster-responding competitors. After setting up automated lead response, their after-hours conversion rate matched their business-hours rate. That's the kind of change that shows up directly in revenue.

The setup isn't complicated, but it needs to be connected to the tools you already use - your CRM, your scheduling software, your phone system. That's where having someone who knows what they're doing matters.

---

## What it doesn't replace

AI doesn't replace your crew, your expertise, or your judgment on the job. It handles the repetitive communication work so your team can focus on actual contracting.
