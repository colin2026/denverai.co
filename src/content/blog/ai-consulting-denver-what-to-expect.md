AI consulting is a loose term. It can mean anything from a freelancer setting up a chatbot to a large agency charging $50k for a strategy deck. If you're a Denver business owner trying to figure out whether it's worth it, here's what you should actually expect.

## What AI consulting should do for your business

A real AI consulting engagement starts with your workflow, not with tools. The first question should be: where is time being lost, and is that loss repeatable enough to automate?

If a consultant leads with "here's what we'll build" before they've spent time understanding your operations, that's a red flag.

Good engagements typically produce:

- A clear map of which processes are worth automating and why
- Working automations connected to your existing software (not new platforms you have to learn)
- Training so your team actually uses what was built
- Measurable time savings within the first few weeks

---

## What it typically costs for a Denver SMB

Pricing varies widely. A few general ranges:

**Project-based work** (building a specific automation or workflow) typically runs $2,000 - $8,000 depending on complexity. This is the right starting point for most businesses.

**Ongoing retainers** (continued optimization, new automations, support) are usually $1,000 - $3,000/month. Makes sense once you've validated the first project.

**Large enterprise engagements** are in a different category - if you're a small business, you shouldn't be paying enterprise rates.

The ROI math is usually straightforward. If you're automating 10 hours of admin work per week at $25/hour, that's $1,000/month in recovered time. A $3,000 project pays for itself in three months.

---

## What separates good AI consultants from bad ones

**They ask about your business before recommending tools.** Any consultant who leads with a specific tool recommendation before understanding your workflow is selling, not consulting.

**They're honest about what AI can't do.** There's a lot of hype right now. Good consultants will tell you which problems AI is genuinely suited for and which ones still need humans.

**They care about adoption, not just implementation.** Software that your team doesn't use is worthless. The best consultants stay involved through training and onboarding, not just delivery.

**They can point to real results.** Ask for specific examples - what did they automate, for what kind of business, and what changed? Vague case studies are a sign they don't have many.

## Why local matters

Working with a Denver-based AI consultant isn't just about convenience - it's about context. Someone who works with local businesses understands the vendor relationships, the staffing realities, and the pace of how Colorado SMBs actually operate.

Remote consultants can build working software. Local consultants can build working software that your team will actually adopt.
